exact inventory. Tests cover all RFC and vendor vectors, every specified

profiles. They carry one unit per octet and reject high bits. The packed facade
preserves the exact non-byte-aligned length and bit order. The separate
`UNIVAC-I-EXPANDED-1959-PAPER-TAPE-ROW` byte codec enforces physical track
order `1,2,3,4,S,5,6,7`, the sprocket bit, and odd parity; it is already an
eight-track transport and therefore is not repacked. FIELDATA MSB packing
matches continuous six-character 36-bit words; LSB packing is an explicit
Iconvex interchange form. The 6/12 grammar reserves units 74 and 76 as escape
prefixes: only 7401, 7402, 7404,
7407, and 7601 through 7677 are defined. The CDC profiles are separate because
the NOS manual assigns different Unicode graphics and different colon/percent
semantics; none is treated as an alias of another.

The byte pipeline also registers `DEC-SPECIAL-GR` and `DEC-TECHNICAL-GR` for
the VT3xx manual's explicit GR invocation. They use octets A1-FE and are not
separate packed profiles: their logical seven-bit values are exactly the GL
profiles above with bit 8 selected by the byte transport.

The separately audited Kermit byte codecs `BULGARIA-PC`, `MAZOVIA`,
`QNX-CONSOLE`, `DG-INTERNATIONAL`, `KERMIT-DG-LINEDRAWING`,
`KERMIT-DG-WORDPROCESSING`, `KERMIT-HP-MATH-TECHNICAL`,
`KERMIT-SNI-BRACKETS`, `KERMIT-SNI-EURO`, `KERMIT-SNI-FACET`, and
`KERMIT-SNI-IBM` are not packed profiles. Each mapped character occupies one
octet. The seven terminal subsets map bytes 21--7E, preserve bytes 00--20, and
reject 7F--FF. Their aliases and exact modules appear in
`SUPPORTED_CODEC_INVENTORY.csv`.

`DEC-HEBREW-8` is a distinct registered byte codec, not a packed spelling of
SI 960. It preserves DEC MCS through BF, rejects C0–DF and FB–FF, and maps
E0–FA to U+05D0–U+05EA. SI 960 rejects every high octet and retains ASCII
outside 60–7A.
